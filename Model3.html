Key Findings:
  • Overall Performance: The “Both Correct” rate is 67.6%, which is lower than that of the top-performing CBOW-based models, indicating a higher overall misclassification rate.
  • Target Level Performance: “Target Wrong Only” accounts for 5.5% of cases. This suggests Skipgram embeddings produce different target classification patterns compared to CBOW models, though target accuracy remains relatively strong.
  • K2 Level Performance: “Sentiment Wrong Only” is 21.9%, showing that sentiment classification remains the primary challenge—consistent with other models using severity as the second level.
  • Embedding Comparison: Skipgram results in a distinct error distribution, with notably higher rates in both “Sentiment Wrong Only” and “Both Wrong” (5.0%) compared to CBOW-based models. This indicates that Skipgram may handle hierarchical label dependencies differently, leading to more combined errors.
